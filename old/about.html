Food Safety & Storage Tips

Fruit Storage

Learn how to properly store different types of fruits to maintain freshness and prevent spoilage.

  • Keep fruits in a cool, dry place
  • Store ripe fruits in the refrigerator
  • Separate ethylene-producing fruits
  • Wash fruits before storage
  • Remove any damaged or spoiled fruits
  • Use breathable containers

Vegetable Storage

Best practices for storing various vegetables to maintain their quality and nutritional value.

  • Keep vegetables in the crisper drawer
  • Remove rubber bands and ties
  • Store root vegetables in a cool, dark place
  • Keep leafy greens in breathable bags
  • Separate ethylene-sensitive vegetables
  • Clean vegetables before storage

Meat & Seafood Handling

Safe steps in meat and seafood handling, cooking, and storage to prevent foodborne illness.

  • Store at proper temperature
  • Use separate cutting boards
  • Cook to recommended temperatures
  • Keep raw meat separate from other foods
  • Thaw meat in the refrigerator
  • Wash hands after handling raw meat

Poultry Storage

Essential tips for storing and handling poultry products safely.

General Food Safety

General food safety guidelines for maintaining a healthy kitchen.

  • Keep kitchen surfaces clean and sanitized
  • Use separate cutting boards for different foods
  • Wash hands frequently while cooking
  • Check expiration dates regularly
  • Maintain proper refrigerator temperature
  • Store food in appropriate containers
